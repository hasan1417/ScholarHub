What the AI Can Do for You11 / 20

Four capability groups
the assistant reaches for.

Instead of a single text generator, the assistant composes capabilities. It decides what to read, what to fetch, and what to draft — and it always reports what it used.

SEARCH
Find papers — inside and outside your library.
Semantic search across the project, or reach out to the 9 external sources when the library doesn't cover it.
LIBRARY
Add, annotate, and analyze references.
Pull in promising papers, attach notes, extract key claims — so later questions have more to work with.
AUTHORING
Draft and edit with citations attached.
Generate a section, propose an edit to a passage, write an abstract — always with the source papers linked.
ANALYSIS
Compare, refine, and find gaps.
Compare two papers, sharpen a research question, surface what's missing from your literature coverage.

Four things that make
ScholarHub different.

CONTRIBUTION 01
The AI actually knows your project.
Your library, your discussions, your documents — every answer is grounded in the papers and context you've already gathered, not a generic model talking into thin air.
CONTRIBUTION 02
Citations that always exist.
The AI can only cite papers already in your library. No fabricated DOIs, no made-up authors, nothing to chase down in Scholar after the fact.
CONTRIBUTION 03
One search instead of nine tabs.
Nine databases merged into a single ranked result list — deduped across sources. Promote what's useful; dismiss the noise. The ranking adapts to your choices.

Every AI suggestion arrives as a reviewable diff with its source citations attached. Accept or reject passage by passage — your prose stays yours.

Trust & Your Data18 / 20

Before you paste
your unpublished draft.

Four questions we expect you to ask — with straight answers.

QUESTION 01
Who can see what I write?
Only members you invite to the project. Four roles — Owner, Admin, Editor, Viewer — decide who edits vs. who just reads. Papers you haven't shared stay with you.
QUESTION 02
Does the AI send my paper to OpenAI?
Bring your own OpenRouter or OpenAI key — encrypted at rest (AES-128), used only for your requests. Your paper goes through your key, billed to your account.
QUESTION 03
What context does the AI have?
Only the project you're working in — its library, documents, and discussions. Other projects never enter the prompt.
QUESTION 04
Can I take my work with me?
Your LaTeX source is copyable, and the BibTeX of any reference is exportable. A one-click project bundle (source + PDF + refs) is on the near roadmap.
